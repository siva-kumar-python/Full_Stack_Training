CO

Computer Organization refers to the operational
structure and functionality of a computer system's hardware
components. It deals with how different parts like the CPU,
memory, input/output devices, and buses work together to execute
instructions. Understanding computer organization helps in grasping
how software interacts with hardware, including concepts like instruction
cycles, data flow, and control signals. It forms the foundation for
designing efficient and optimized computer systems.
